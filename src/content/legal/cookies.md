## 1. What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They allow the site to remember your actions and preferences (such as login state, language, and display settings) over time, so you don't have to re-enter them on every visit.

## 2. How we use cookies

DSO Hire LLC uses cookies and similar technologies (pixels, local storage, session storage) on https://dsohire.com to:

- Keep you signed in to your account between visits
- Remember your search filters and viewing preferences
- Measure how visitors use the Services so we can improve them
- Detect and prevent fraud, abuse, and security issues
- Optionally, evaluate the effectiveness of marketing campaigns

## 3. Types of cookies we use

### 3.1 Strictly necessary cookies

Required for the Services to function. They are set in response to actions you take, such as logging in or filling out a form. You cannot opt out of strictly necessary cookies — without them, the Services will not work.

### 3.2 Performance and analytics cookies

Help us understand how the Services are used (which pages are popular, how long visitors stay, whether errors occur). We may use providers such as PostHog, Plausible, or similar analytics platforms that respect Do Not Track signals.

### 3.3 Functional cookies

Remember choices you make to give you a better experience — such as your language, your saved searches, or whether you've dismissed a notification banner.

### 3.4 Targeting / advertising cookies (limited use)

If we run paid marketing on platforms such as LinkedIn or Google, those platforms may set cookies that allow them to measure ad effectiveness. We do not sell personal information for advertising and do not engage in targeted advertising as those terms are defined under US state privacy laws.

## 4. Managing cookies

You can control cookies in several ways:

- Browser settings — most browsers let you block or delete cookies, including third-party cookies. Blocking strictly necessary cookies will prevent the Services from working.
- Do Not Track and Global Privacy Control (GPC) signals — we honor GPC signals as required by applicable law
- Account settings — when you are signed in, you can control marketing communications and certain analytics preferences in your account
- Industry opt-outs — for ad-related cookies, see http://optout.aboutads.info and http://optout.networkadvertising.org

## 5. Changes to this policy

We may update this Cookie Policy when we add or remove tracking technologies. The updated policy will be posted with a new effective date.
